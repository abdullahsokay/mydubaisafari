A desert safari is the single experience most Dubai visitors rate as the highlight of their trip. But whether you're booked on an [evening desert safari](/tours/shared-desert-safari) or a [morning desert safari](/tours/morning-desert-safari), wearing the wrong clothes can turn an extraordinary adventure into an uncomfortable one. This guide covers exactly what to pack and wear — so all you need to worry about is enjoying the dunes.

## Why What You Wear Actually Matters

The Arabian Desert is a dramatic environment. Daytime temperatures in summer can exceed 45°C; winter mornings can feel brisk at 12–15°C before 8 am. Fine sand works its way into everything. Dune bashing shakes you around in a 4x4 — and sandboarding means planting yourself on sliding dunes. The right clothing protects you from the sun, keeps you cool or warm as needed, and simply makes the whole experience more enjoyable.

## What to Wear on a Morning Desert Safari



Morning safaris typically run from around 7–8 am to noon. The early hours are cool — sometimes genuinely cold in winter — before heating up quickly by mid-morning.

### Clothing

- **Light layers:** Start with a long-sleeve T-shirt or light shirt that you can roll up as it warms. A thin fleece or zip-up is useful in winter months (November–February).
- **Trousers or leggings:** Lightweight breathable trousers (linen or cotton) are ideal. They protect your legs from the sun and from sand abrasions when sandboarding.
- **Avoid dark colours:** Black and navy absorb heat dramatically. Opt for whites, creams, tans, or light greys.

### Footwear

Closed trainers or canvas shoes are your best option. You'll take them off for sandboarding — leave them with the guide. Avoid sandals; your feet will blister on hot sand.

### Accessories

- **Sunglasses** — polarised lenses are best in the bright desert light
- **Wide-brimmed hat or cap** — essential sun protection
- **Lightweight scarf or buff** — protects your neck and can cover your face during windy moments
- **Sunscreen SPF 50+** — apply before you board the vehicle

## What to Wear on an Evening Desert Safari


Evening safaris typically depart around 3–4 pm and return at 9–10 pm. The afternoon sun is intense, but temperatures drop pleasantly after sunset.

### Clothing

- **Breathable afternoon outfit:** A loose cotton shirt or linen shirt with shorts or lightweight trousers works perfectly for the dune-bashing and sunset photography portion.
- **Warm layer for the evening:** The desert gets significantly cooler after sunset — sometimes dropping 10–15°C within an hour. Bring a light jacket, hoodie, or pashmina that fits in your bag.
- **Camp dinner comfort:** The Bedouin camp is relaxed. Whatever you're comfortable sitting cross-legged in is perfect.

### Footwear

Same as morning safaris — closed shoes that you can easily slip off for the dunes. The sand cools quickly after sunset, making barefoot dune walking magical.

## What to Pack in Your Bag

Beyond clothing, these items will significantly improve your experience:

| Item | Why You Need It |
|---|---|
| Refillable water bottle | Hydration is critical; operators provide water but extras are welcome |
| Camera or phone with good camera | Sunset dune photos are iconic — make sure your battery is charged |
| Portable charger/power bank | No charging points in the desert |
| Small cash (AED) | For henna, camel rides, or additional drinks at camp |
| Insect repellent | Mostly unnecessary but useful near the camp in summer |
| Wet wipes | Sand gets everywhere — freshen up before the camp dinner |

## What NOT to Bring

- High heels or platform shoes — completely impractical
- Expensive jewellery — sand and activity are not kind to it
- Large handbags — a small crossbody or daypack is far more practical
- Excessive electronics — the dunes are dusty; protect what you can't afford to lose

## A Note on Photography Clothing

If Instagram-worthy dune shots are high on your list, earth tones — camel, terracotta, burnt orange, cream — photograph beautifully against the golden sand. Flowing fabrics catch the wind perfectly for dramatic shots. Many of our guests plan an outfit specifically for the dunes — and it's absolutely worth it.
