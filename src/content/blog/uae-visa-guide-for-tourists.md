Dubai is one of the world's most visited cities, and for good reason — but navigating the UAE's visa rules before you book can feel confusing. This guide cuts through the complexity and tells you exactly what applies to you in 2025, whether you're arriving from London, New York, Mumbai, or anywhere in between.

## Who Can Enter the UAE Without a Visa?

The UAE operates one of the most generous visa-on-arrival programmes in the region. Citizens of the following country groups generally receive a **free 30-day visa on arrival**:

- **All EU member states** (Austria, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, etc.)
- **United Kingdom** — 30 days on arrival, extendable
- **United States & Canada** — 30 days on arrival
- **Australia & New Zealand** — 30 days on arrival
- **Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Hong Kong** — 30 days on arrival
- **GCC citizens** (Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, Qatar) — unlimited entry as residents of the wider Gulf region

> **Important:** Visa regulations change. Always verify your eligibility through the official UAE Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship (ICA) portal at **icp.gov.ae** or confirm with your airline before travelling.

## Who Needs a UAE Tourist Visa?

Travellers from countries not on the visa-on-arrival list must apply for a UAE tourist visa in advance. Common nationalities that require advance visas include citizens of India, Pakistan, the Philippines, Nigeria, Egypt, and many others.

### How to Apply for a UAE Tourist Visa

There are three main channels:

**1. UAE ICA eVisa Portal (Official)**
Apply directly at **icp.gov.ae**. You'll need:
- A valid passport (minimum 6 months validity remaining)
- A passport-size photo with white background
- Return flight details
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking)
- A debit or credit card for the fee

Processing typically takes 3–5 working days. Approval is sent by email.

**2. Through Your Airline**
Emirates, flydubai, Air Arabia, and Etihad Airways can process UAE visas for passengers on their flights. This is often the easiest option if you are flying on one of these carriers. Visa fees are added to your booking or paid separately.

**3. Through a Licensed Travel Agent or Tour Operator**
Authorised tour operators like MyDubaiSafari can arrange visa applications as part of a holiday package, often streamlining the process and providing support if issues arise.

## Visa Rules & Important Conditions

### Passport Validity
Your passport must be valid for at least **6 months** beyond your intended travel dates. Many airlines will not board passengers whose passports expire within 6 months of travel.

### Entry Requirements on Arrival
Even with a valid visa or on-arrival eligibility, UAE border officers may request:
- Proof of onward/return travel (a return ticket)
- Proof of sufficient funds
- Accommodation details (hotel booking confirmation)

Having these documents accessible on your phone or printed is strongly advised.

### Prohibited Items
The UAE has strict customs regulations. Certain medications (including codeine-based painkillers and some antidepressants) require a medical certificate. Recreational drugs, certain food items, and explicit materials are prohibited. Check the UAE customs authority website for the full list.

### Travel Insurance
While not legally required for a UAE visa, comprehensive travel insurance covering medical emergencies, cancellations, and lost luggage is strongly recommended. Medical care in Dubai is world-class but expensive without insurance.

## Arriving in Dubai

Dubai International Airport (DXB) handles immigration efficiently. EU/UK/US passport holders clear the e-gate in under 5 minutes. Travellers requiring visa-on-arrival receive a stamp at the immigration counter — the process is generally straightforward and well-staffed.

On departure, you'll pass through the same terminal's emigration section, where your departure is recorded and your visa is marked as used.
