There is a moment, balanced at the top of a 30-metre red dune with a sandboard under your feet and the Dubai desert stretching to the horizon, when you wonder how sandboarding is not the most famous activity in the UAE. Then you push off, the board accelerates across the face of the dune, sand sprays up around you, and the question answers itself: this is spectacular.

Sandboarding is included on many Dubai desert safari packages and is genuinely accessible to beginners. No experience required. No snow required. Just a dune, a board, and a willingness to climb back up for another run.

## How Sandboarding Works

A sandboard looks like a snowboard but is specifically built (or modified) for sand. Key differences include:

- **Board base:** Sandboards use a harder, smoother base material (often formica or laminex) that reduces friction against sand particles, which are more abrasive than snow crystals
- **Wax:** The board base is regularly waxed with a paraffin-based wax before each session to maintain speed — without wax, sand friction slows the board significantly
- **Bindings (optional):** For standing sandboarding, bindings hold your feet in position. Prone boarding (lying down) does not require bindings

### The Two Styles

**Prone boarding (lying face down):** This is the recommended starting point for beginners. You lie on the board, chest down, and push off from the dune's crest. Steering is done by shifting your weight and dragging a foot. Speeds are exhilarating and the risk of injury is minimal since you are already close to the surface.

**Standing boarding:** Once you are comfortable prone, guides can set up bindings for a standing run. The technique closely resembles snowboarding — weight on the back foot to initiate, edge control to turn. The learning curve is steeper, but the feeling of carving down a dune face while standing is extraordinary.

## Where Do You Sandboard in Dubai?

Desert safari operators in Dubai use dunes in the Dubai Desert Conservation Reserve and the Al Lahbab "Big Red" dune area southeast of the city. The best dunes for sandboarding are those with:

- A steep enough face to generate speed (typically 20–30+ degree incline)
- A wide, open run-out at the bottom with no rocks or obstacles
- Consistent sand texture — fine-grained compacted faces work better than loose powder

Your safari guide will assess dune conditions on the day and select the appropriate face for the group's experience level.

## Step-by-Step: Your First Sandboard Run

1. **The briefing:** Your guide explains weight distribution, braking (drag your feet or hands in the sand), and how to bail safely if you lose control (aim to roll, not to grab the board)
2. **The climb:** Walk up the selected dune face. Take it slowly — loose sand requires effort. Use a diagonal line if the face is steep
3. **Board positioning:** Place the board perpendicular to the slope at the crest. Lie prone with your chest on the board, legs out behind
4. **The push:** Either push yourself off with your hands, or ask your guide to give you a starting push
5. **The run:** Keep your weight centred, head up, and enjoy the acceleration. Use your feet to steer by pressing into the sand on either side
6. **The stop:** At the bottom, drag both feet simultaneously into the sand for a firm stop. Or, if speed is too high, lean slightly to one side and allow the board to turn across the slope

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Sandboarding

- **Wax the board before every run** — guides handle this, but it makes a significant difference to speed
- **Start on a smaller dune** to build confidence before tackling the steepest face available
- **Loosen your shoes** slightly so sand can exit rather than building up inside
- **Stay hydrated** — the climbing between runs in desert heat is more physically demanding than it looks
- **Do not fight the fall** — when you wipe out (and you will, at least once), let yourself roll rather than bracing with your wrists
