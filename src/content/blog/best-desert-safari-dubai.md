A **desert safari in Dubai** is the single most iconic experience the emirate offers — trading the glass skyline for endless red dunes, sunset silhouettes, and a night of Bedouin hospitality under the stars. If you only do one thing outside the city, this is it. This complete guide covers every type of Dubai desert safari, what's included, real prices, the best time to go, and how to choose the right package so you book with confidence.

## What Is a Desert Safari?

A desert safari is a guided trip into the Arabian desert that combines adventure and culture in a single outing. You are collected from your hotel in a 4x4, driven out to the dunes for **dune bashing**, and then hosted at a desert camp for dinner, entertainment, and traditional activities. It is part adrenaline, part cultural evening, and completely unforgettable — which is exactly why it tops nearly every list of the [top things to do in Dubai](/blog/top-10-things-to-do-in-dubai).

## Types of Desert Safari in Dubai

Not all safaris are the same. Choosing the right *type* matters more than any other decision.

### Evening Desert Safari

The classic and most popular option. You head out in the late afternoon, catch the sunset over the dunes, then spend the evening at camp with a BBQ buffet, live shows, and stargazing. Best for first-timers, families, and couples who want the full experience.

### Morning Desert Safari

A shorter, cooler, adventure-focused trip — perfect for photographers and anyone who prefers to avoid the crowds. If you are torn between the two, our detailed [morning vs evening desert safari comparison](/blog/morning-vs-evening-desert-safari) breaks down exactly who each one suits.

### Private & VIP Desert Safari

Your own vehicle, your own guide, and often a private or premium camp. Ideal for honeymoons, family groups, corporate outings, and [private desert events and celebrations](/blog/private-desert-events-and-celebrations). You trade the shared-camp buzz for space, comfort, and flexibility.

### Overnight Camp Safari

Sleep in the desert. After the evening programme winds down, guests stay in a Bedouin-style camp and wake to a quiet sunrise over the dunes — the most immersive way to experience the [Dubai desert camp](/blog/desert-camp-bbq-dinner-and-shows).

## What's Included in a Desert Safari

While packages vary, a standard Dubai desert safari includes:

- **Hotel pickup and drop-off** in an air-conditioned 4x4
- **Dune bashing** — the signature thrill ride across the dunes
- **Camel rides** through the desert ([more on camel riding](/blog/camel-riding-in-the-dubai-desert))
- **Sandboarding** down the steeper dune faces ([sandboarding guide](/blog/sandboarding-in-the-dubai-desert))
- **A BBQ dinner buffet** with vegetarian and halal options
- **Live entertainment** — tanoura, belly dancing, and a fire show
- **Henna, shisha, Arabic coffee, and dates**
- **Optional add-ons** like quad bikes and dune buggies ([buggy vs quad guide](/blog/dune-buggy-vs-quad-bike-dubai))

## Best Time for a Desert Safari

Dubai is a year-round destination, but the desert is most comfortable from **October to April**, when daytime temperatures are pleasant and evenings are cool. Summer safaris (May–September) still run, usually in the cooler evening hours. For a month-by-month breakdown, see our guide to the [best time to visit Dubai](/blog/best-time-to-visit-dubai).

## How Much Does a Desert Safari Cost in Dubai?

Prices depend on the type of safari and your group size:

- **Shared evening safari:** from around **AED 150 per person**
- **Private safari:** from roughly **AED 650–750** for two people
- **Premium & VIP camps:** from **AED 799–1,499** depending on exclusivity and dining

## What to Wear and Bring

Light, breathable clothing and closed shoes work best, with a layer for the cooler evening. Bring sunglasses, sunscreen, and a fully charged phone or camera — the light out there is spectacular. ## How to Choose the Best Desert Safari

1. **Decide on shared vs private** — budget and crowd tolerance usually settle this.
2. **Pick your time** — evening for the full experience, morning for adventure and photos.
3. **Check what's actually included** — camp quality and dinner vary a lot between operators.
4. **Book with a licensed operator** — safety and reliability matter in the desert.

Absolutely. Few experiences pack this much variety — adventure, culture, food, and scenery — into a single evening, at a price that suits almost any budget. Whether it is your first trip or your fifth, the dunes never lose their magic.
